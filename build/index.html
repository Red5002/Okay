About Song

My name is Ijoko Ode Jairus, but the world knows me as Flexy J, aka ODEE. I’m not just a music artist—I’m the lyrical poem that expresses the thoughts and feelings of a writer. My voice is the sound of survival, my rhythm is the heartbeat of resilience, and my message is simple: It’s OKAY .

I wrote “OKAY” from the deepest part of my soul. This song was born in the fire of loss—when I lost my father, my life changed forever. Everything fell apart. Feeding became a struggle. Shelter became a dream. My family had no place to truly call home.

I was too young to have the conversations I needed to have with my dad, and I grew up with that ache. But I turned that pain into power. I became the man I needed, and I used my voice to speak what so many are feeling but afraid to say.

I’ve lived the street life—I sold pure water in the streets of Lagos. I carried loads in the market. I did Okada business. I hustled, not because I wanted to, but because I had to. I did all of that just to keep the spirit alive.

People will call you names. In Yoruba, they’ll say Omoale, Omoase, Oloriburuku. But I’ve learned something from the ocean: to rejoice even when you’re drowning. Because the storm will pass.

“OKAY” is not just a song. It’s a lifeline. It’s my message to the world—to the ones who are struggling, to the ones who feel forgotten, to the ones who cry in silence: Don’t give up. Keep pushing. It’s OK to not be OK. Your story isn’t over. The sky is not your limit; it’s your starting point.

I am ODEE, I am the living proof that pain births purpose, and I’m using my voice to light up dark paths. This is more than music. This is a movement.
